Internal Design Review

Why Marketing's Cyan Light Mode
Won't Work for ALON

A detailed analysis of the "Light Edition Brand Guide v1.0" (Cyan #14B8A6 accent) versus ALON's Trust Architecture palette — with logic, real scenarios, and side-by-side screen comparisons.

8 Counterpoints with Logic & Scenarios

1Brand Split: Two Apps in One
The marketing guide uses Cyan #14B8A6 as the primary accent while our app uses Terracotta #D95F2B. These are not variations of the same hue — they're on opposite sides of the color wheel (warm vs cool). When a user sees a terracotta CTA on dark mode and a cyan CTA on light mode, they experience two completely different brands.
Scenario: A user downloads ALON and opens it at night (dark mode). They see warm terracotta buttons, the familiar ALON eye with its orange iris. Next morning, the phone auto-switches to light mode. Suddenly every button is teal, the entire emotional tone has shifted. They wonder: "Did the app update? Is this the same app?" This is brand confusion, not a theme variant.
2Cyan = SaaS / Fintech, Not Real Estate
Cyan/teal (#14B8A6) is the default accent of Tailwind CSS, Stripe, Notion, Vercel, Linear, Slack, and a hundred SaaS dashboards. It screams "productivity software." Real estate is an emotional, high-stakes, trust-heavy purchase — the biggest financial decision most Indians make. Terracotta evokes earth, brick, home. Cyan evokes cloud, code, efficiency.
Scenario: A homebuyer opens ALON alongside Housing.com and NoBroker. If ALON uses cyan, it looks like a tech dashboard, not a property companion. Terracotta creates an instant category signal — "this app understands homes, not just data."
3ALON Avatar Identity Breaks
The ALON avatar — our brand mascot — uses a terracotta iris (#D95F2B), terracotta-tinted rings, and warm amber glow. This is deeply embedded across every screen. In the cyan scheme, the avatar either keeps its terracotta eye (clashing with every cyan element around it) or changes to a cyan eye (destroying the established character identity).
Scenario: The ALON eye sits in the dashboard header, in chat bubbles, on the splash screen, in marketing materials. If we use cyan for light mode, the eye becomes orphaned warm in a cold UI, or we maintain two avatar variants with different eye colors — confusing the one visual element that IS the brand.
4Cool Gray Surfaces Kill Warmth
The marketing guide uses #F8FAFC (cool blue-gray) for surfaces. Our Trust Architecture uses #F5F0E8 (warm cream). This difference isn't subtle — it shifts the entire emotional register. Cool grays feel clinical and transactional. Warm creams feel welcoming and residential. In the context of browsing properties — places you'll call home — warmth matters.
Scenario: A user is browsing property cards on the preferences screen. With warm cream backgrounds (#F5F0E8), the unselected pills feel like paper, like a physical document. With cool gray (#F8FAFC), the same pills feel like a spreadsheet cell. The same content, completely different emotional frame.
5Trust Architecture Was a Deliberate Choice
We chose the Prussian Navy + Terracotta palette specifically to solve a design tension unique to ALON: bridging "AI-cold" (the agent, verification, data) with "purchase-warm" (home, family, investment). Cyan doubles down on the cold side — it says "I am a tool" instead of "I understand your home journey." The Trust Architecture palette was designed to signal: "Yes, I'm intelligent AND I care about your biggest purchase."
Scenario: During the 8-stage journey, ALON moves from analytical (search, shortlist) to deeply personal (negotiation, possession). Terracotta carries emotional weight across all stages. Cyan feels fine for "Search" but becomes tonally inappropriate for "Possession" — the moment you get your home keys shouldn't feel like completing a Jira ticket.
6Navy Inconsistency (#0F172A vs #0D1F4A)
The marketing guide uses #0F172A (Slate 900 from Tailwind) as their navy. Our codebase uses #0D1F4A (Prussian navy — a warmer, bluer, more distinctive dark). These are different navies. #0F172A is a generic dark that appears in thousands of websites. #0D1F4A is a curated Prussian navy that pairs specifically with terracotta for the "institutional trust" signal.
Scenario: If we ship with the marketing's Slate 900, our dark header (#0F172A) on light mode looks like every Tailwind starter template. Our Prussian navy (#0D1F4A) is recognizably ALON — even a 2-pixel header stripe would identify the brand.
7Selection States Lose Meaning
In our current system, terracotta = user's choice/action and blue = system/interactive state. This creates a clear semantic distinction. The cyan guide makes cyan = everything — CTAs, highlights, accents, selected pills. When one color does everything, nothing has semantic meaning. Users can't quickly distinguish "I chose this" from "the system suggests this."
Scenario: On the preferences screen, selected property types (user action) should feel different from ALON's AI suggestions (system intelligence). With Trust Architecture, terracotta = your choice, blue = ALON's pick. With cyan for everything, there's no visual language for this distinction — and we lose one of our strongest UX signals.
8Indian Market Differentiation
Every major Indian proptech app — Housing.com, 99acres, NoBroker, Square Yards — uses blues, greens, or teals. Cyan puts ALON directly into that visual cluster. Terracotta is unprecedented in Indian proptech. In a market where brand recall drives repeat usage, looking like everyone else is the fastest way to be forgotten.
Scenario: A buyer has 4 property apps installed. They're all some shade of blue/teal. ALON with terracotta is the only warm icon on their home screen. Without looking at labels, they know which one is ALON. With cyan, we become the fifth blue icon.

Recommendation

Trust Architecture is the Only Viable Light Mode

The cyan palette isn't "wrong" in isolation — it's a perfectly fine SaaS color scheme. But ALON isn't a SaaS product. It's a real estate companion for the most emotional purchase of someone's life. The Trust Architecture palette (Prussian Navy + Terracotta + Warm Cream) maintains brand consistency across themes, carries emotional warmth into light surfaces, differentiates ALON in the Indian proptech market, and preserves the semantic color language that makes the app intuitive.

Action: Continue with Trust Architecture for light mode. White backgrounds (#FFFFFF), warm cream surfaces (#F5F0E8), Prussian navy text (#0D1F4A), terracotta CTAs (#D95F2B). No need for a separate "light palette" — the Trust Architecture IS the light palette.
